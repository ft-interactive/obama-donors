Hillary Clinton war chest brace for flood

By Jennifer Bissell and Courtney Weaver  |  JUNE 22, 2016
  • Twitter
  • Facebook
  • LinkedIn

FT/Crowdpac analysis reveals a quarter of Barack Obama's top 200 donors have yet to give

The war chest funding Hillary Clinton’s campaign is bracing itself for an influx of donations with her coronation as the Democratic party’s presumptive nominee.

Throughout the primary season the party remained divided as she and Bernie Sanders battled for the nomination. But as Mrs Clinton heads into the general election against Donald Trump as the Republican nominee, more Democrats are expected to open their pocket books.

At the start of the election, it was predicted both the Democrat and Republican candidates would spend at least $1bn. Mr Trump has rejected the need to spend that amount. But Mrs Clinton and the Democratic National Committee could still surpass the $1.2bn spent in 2012 by Barack Obama and the DNC’s joint fundraiser on top of the $75.3m spent by his super-Pac.

By June during the 2012 election, Mr Obama, the DNC and his super-Pac had raised a combined $456.5m, according to FEC filings. The funds backing Mrs Clinton, likewise, has raised at least $386.9m, despite the fierce competition from Mr Sanders for individual donations.

Mr Sanders had secured more donations from Mr Obama’s small, grassroot donors, allowing him to out-fundraise Mrs Clinton’s principal campaign fund. However Mrs Clinton has had the support of more wealthy and generous donors, many of which also supported Mr Obama during his 2008 and 2012 campaigns, according to an FT analysis in collaboration with Crowdpac.

Of Mr Obama’s top 200 financial backers, about two-thirds (69%) have already given to Mrs Clinton, while the remaining quarter (28%) have yet to give to either Mrs Clinton or Mr Sanders.

The list of contributors sitting on the sidelines so far features many prominent Democratic donors, including Anne Cox Chambers, the 96-year-old media proprietor, Mary and David Boies, a founding partner of law firm Boies, Schiller & Flexner, and Sidney Kimmel, the founder of Jones Apparel Group and the production company behind Death at a Funeral.

Of those who have donated, most have given significantly less, including roughly 80 who have given less than 5 per cent as much.

Yet that is not to say all of Mr Obama’s highest donors like her less. Hedge fund manager George Soros, who also founded the MoveOn.org Pac, has given funds backing Mrs Clinton at least $7.4m this year, while he gave just over $1m during Mr Obama’s 2008 and 2012 campaigns. Similarly Ewa and Daniel Abraham, the founder of Slim-Fast, and hedge fund manager James Simon have given $2m more to funds backing Mrs Clinton than they gave to funds backing Mr Obama.

Mr Obama's top three donors

Sums include contributions to Mr Obama's 2012 and Mrs Clinton's 2016 principal campaigns, DNC joint fundraisers and super-Pacs backing their candidacy.

James and Marilyn Simons

With an estimated net worth of $15.5bn, Mr Simons ranks as Forbes’ 26th richest billionaire in the US and the second highest earning hedge fund manager. He stepped down as chief executive from his fund, Renaissance Technologies, in 2010 while Mrs Simons continues to serve as president of the Simons Foundation, one of the largest charities advancing math and science research.

Fred Eychaner

Mr Eychaner is a retired media executive from Chicago who founded Newsweb, a newspaper printing company. He is a noted donor for gay rights groups and HIV/AIDS prevention, and is also the founder of the Alphawood, a grant-making foundation.

Jeffrey and Marilyn Katzenberg

Mr Katzenberg served as the chairman of Walt Disney for 10 years before cofounding Dreamworks Animation and serving as its chief executive. He and his wife have an estimated fortune of $750m, according to Forbes.

Mrs Clinton's top three donors

Sums include contributions to Mr Obama's 2012 and Mrs Clinton's 2016 principal campaigns, DNC joint fundraisers and super-Pacs backing their candidacy.

Haim and Cheryl Saban

Mr Saban is the founder of a children’s TV production company best known for Power Rangers. With an estimated fortune of $3.6bn, he and his wife are major donors for pro-Israel and Jewish causes. Ms Saban is also the founder of the Self Worth Foundation for Women and Girls.

JB and MK Pritzker

Jay Robert Pritzker is an heir to the Pritzker family who is known for owning Hyatt Hotels and the Marmon Group. Mr Pritzker, who goes by JB, chairs the ChicagoNext startup center and is the managing partner of the private investment firm Pritzker Group. He and his wife, Mary Kathryn lead the Pritzker Family Foundation. Their wealth is estimated at $3.3bn according to Forbes.

George Soros

An investing heavyweight, Mr Soros is the 15th richest billionaire in the US and ranks as the 10th highest paid hedge fund manager. At 85 years old, he has donated $11bn over his lifetime, primarily through his Open Society foundation.
