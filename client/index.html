Hillary Clinton war chest brace for flood

By Jennifer Bissell and Courtney Weaver  |  MARCH 7, 2016
  • Twitter
  • Facebook
  • LinkedIn

FT/Crowdpac analysis reveals a third of Barack Obama's top 200 donors have yet to give

The war chest funding Hillary Clinton’s campaign is bracing itself for an influx of donations with her coronation as the Democratic party’s presumptive nominee.

Throughout the primary season the party remained divided as she and Bernie Sanders battled for the nomination. But as Mrs Clinton heads into the general election against Donald Trump as the Republican nominee, more Democrats are expected to open their pocket books.

At the start of the election, it was predicted both the Democrat and Republican candidates would spend at least $1bn. Mr Trump has rejected the need to spend that amount. But Mrs Clinton could still surpass the $1.2bn spent in 2012 by Barack Obama and the Democratic National Committee’s fundraising efforts on top of the $75.3m spent by his super-Pac.

By April during the 2012 election, individuals had donated roughly $268m to the major funds backing Mr Obama, according to political data start-up Crowdpac. Mrs Clinton, likewise, has raised $257m despite the fierce competition from Mr Sanders for individual donations.

Mr Sanders had secured more donations from Mr Obama’s small, grassroot donors, allowing him to out-fundraise Mrs Clinton’s principal campaign fund. However Mrs Clinton has had the support of more wealthy and generous donors, many of which also supported Mr Obama during both his 2008 and 2012 campaigns, according to an FT analysis in collaboration with Crowdpac.

Of Mr Obama’s top 200 financial backers, about two-thirds (68%) have already given to Mrs Clinton, while the remaining quarter (28%) have yet to give to either Mrs Clinton or Mr Sanders.

The list of contributors sitting on the sidelines so far features many prominent Democratic donors, including Anne Cox Chambers, the 96-year-old media proprietor, David Boies, a founding partner of law firm Boies, Schiller & Flexner, and Sidney Kimmel, the founder of Jones Apparel Group and the production company behind Death at a Funeral.

Of those who have donated, most have given significantly less, including roughly 80 who have given less than 5 per cent as much.

Yet that is not to say all of Mr Obama’s highest donors like her less. Hedge fund manager George Soros, who also founded the MoveOn.org Pac, has given funds backing Mrs Clinton at least $7.4m this year, while he gave just over $1m during Mr Obama’s 2008 and 2012 campaigns. Similarly Ewa and Daniel Abraham, the founder of Slim-Fast, and hedge fund manager James Simon have given at least 40 per cent more than the million dollar donations they gave to Mr Obama.
